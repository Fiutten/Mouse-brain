\section{Research contributions}

The contributions of the proposal are summarised in the next points:

\begin{itemize}
  \item The main innovation of MouseBrainBench lies in its claim-audit
  formulation. The framework does not evaluate a digital mouse-brain model using
  a unique predictive score, but separates reproducibility, prediction,
  anatomical control, directionality, structure-function association, and
  computational cost into different evidence blocks. This prevents a predictive
  result from being interpreted as mechanistic evidence without the corresponding
  controls.

  \item A Mechanistic Identifiability Score is proposed as a conservative
  decision layer. This score is not designed to reward complexity, but to decide
  whether a model has enough evidence to support a specific mechanistic claim.
  Thus, a model can be useful and predictive while being rejected as a causal or
  mechanistically identifiable explanation.

  \item The proposed architecture integrates heterogeneous public resources in a
  reproducible manner. Allen Visual Behavior Neuropixels, Sensorium static,
  Dynamic Sensorium, and MICRONS CAVE are not mixed as equivalent evidence, but
  used with specific roles according to the type of scientific question that
  each resource can support.

  \item The framework includes negative, predictive, and positive empirical
  cases. Allen is used to show that reproducible neural signals are not
  sufficient to infer mechanism; Sensorium is used to evaluate predictive models
  without upgrading them to causal explanations; and MICRONS is used as a local
  structure-function case with anatomical and functional data from the same
  cortical resource.

  \item A replicated MICRONS endpoint is provided. Synaptically connected
  directed pairs show closer readout-location similarity than distance- and
  degree-matched non-connected controls across a discovery cohort and two
  independent hold-out cohorts. This result supports a local observational
  structure-function association, while explicitly blocking whole-brain,
  behavioural, and causal digital-twin claims.
\end{itemize}
