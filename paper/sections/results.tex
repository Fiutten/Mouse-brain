\section{Results}

\subsection{Synthetic known-truth cases validate the gate logic}

The synthetic benchmark tests whether the Mechanistic Identifiability Score
accepts only the designed directed-truth case. Common-drive, structure-only, and
timing-only controls isolate failure modes that can otherwise be mistaken for
mechanistic evidence. These experiments are methods validation rather than
empirical evidence about mouse brain data.

\subsection{Allen VBN is reproducible but not mechanistically identifiable}

The Allen VBN analysis passes reproducibility criteria but fails topology
specificity and directed-identifiability criteria. Median cross-mouse
correlation is 0.891 and median split-half correlation is 0.990, but anatomical
specificity does not pass: Allen connectivity underperforms the median
permutation by -0.0054 and the paired bootstrap lower bound is negative. The
final decision is therefore \emph{reproducible target without mechanistic
identifiability}.

\subsection{Sensorium supports predictive benchmarking but not causal claims}

Sensorium static provides a positive predictive and reliability layer: in the
five repeated-test mice, median reliability is 0.642 and median best predictive
correlation is 0.341. Dynamic Sensorium provides a temporal stress test. Local
temporal and neural-network baselines improve prediction in several comparisons,
but reliability remains non-estimable in the public dynamic artifacts and the
bounded official Sensorium baseline is not Q1-qualified. MouseBrainBench
therefore treats Sensorium as predictive evidence, not as mechanistic or causal
evidence.

\subsection{The global MICRONS aggregate test blocks an overclaim}

The first MICRONS expanded aggregate test did not survive distance matching,
despite positive random and degree-matched comparisons. This is an important
failure mode: a global positive result can be driven by spatial proximity or
degree structure. The manuscript therefore does not use the aggregate test as
the main positive claim.

\subsection{MICRONS stratified structure-function association replicates}

The primary endpoint is fixed as \texttt{all\_pairs/readout\_location}.
Synaptically connected directed pairs are compared against non-connected pairs
under random, distance-matched, and degree-matched controls. The endpoint is
accepted only if it is positive after matched controls, FDR correction, and
unit-cluster bootstrap stability in discovery and both hold-out cohorts.

Across all three cohorts, connected pairs show closer readout-location
similarity than matched non-connected pairs. The distance-matched delta is
0.0215 in discovery, 0.0210 in the first hold-out, and 0.0179 in the second
hold-out. Unit-cluster bootstrap intervals remain positive in every cohort and
for both distance- and degree-matched controls.

\subsection{Claim audit freezes allowed and blocked interpretations}

The publication freeze marks the replicated local MICRONS structure-function
claim as Q1-candidate evidence. It also blocks unsupported claims: causal
mechanism, complete mouse-brain digital twin, behavioral digital twin, and
Sensorium SOTA predictor. The resulting paper is therefore a benchmark and
claim-audit contribution, not a whole-brain simulation paper.
