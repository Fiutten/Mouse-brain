\section{Experiments and results}

This section presents the experiments carried out to evaluate MouseBrainBench.
The objective is not to show that all datasets support a positive claim, but to
verify that the framework is able to distinguish between different levels of
evidence. For this reason, the experiments include method validation, negative
controls, predictive benchmarks, and a local positive structure-function case.

\subsection{Synthetic experiments}

The first experiment evaluates the Mechanistic Identifiability Score using
synthetic known-truth cases. The score must accept the directed-truth case and
reject common-drive, structure-only, and timing-only alternatives. This process
is necessary because a claim-audit framework should be validated before it is
applied to biological data. The achieved behaviour confirms that the score does
not accept partial evidence as a complete mechanism.

\subsection{Allen Visual Behavior Neuropixels}

The second experiment uses Allen Visual Behavior Neuropixels as a real negative
control. The obtained results show that the dataset contains reproducible
signals. Median cross-mouse correlation is 0.891 and median split-half
correlation is 0.990. However, the anatomical and directed-identifiability gates
do not pass. Allen connectivity underperforms the median permutation by
-0.0054, and the paired bootstrap lower bound is negative. Therefore, the final
decision for this layer is \emph{reproducible target without mechanistic
identifiability}.

This result is important for the proposal because it shows that MouseBrainBench
does not transform reproducibility into mechanism. A less restrictive benchmark
could interpret the high correlations as positive evidence. In contrast, the
proposed framework accepts the reproducibility result and rejects the
mechanistic claim.

\subsection{Sensorium and Dynamic Sensorium}

The third group of experiments evaluates predictive layers. Sensorium static
provides a positive predictive and reliability case. In the five repeated-test
mice, median reliability is 0.642 and median best predictive correlation is
0.341. Thus, this resource is useful to compare predictive models under
repeated observations.

Dynamic Sensorium provides a more demanding temporal scenario. Local temporal
and neural-network baselines improve prediction in several paired comparisons.
In the current public mice, the best transparent models reach correlations
between 0.431 and 0.526 depending on the mouse. In the legacy
out-of-distribution mice, the best models reach correlations between 0.360 and
0.449. Nevertheless, reliability remains non-estimable in the current public
dynamic artifacts, and the bounded official Sensorium baseline is not
leaderboard-equivalent. Therefore, Dynamic Sensorium is considered predictive
evidence, but not mechanistic or causal evidence.

\subsection{MICRONS aggregate control}

The fourth experiment evaluates an expanded MICRONS aggregate structure-function
test. This first global approach did not survive distance matching, although it
showed positive random and degree-matched comparisons. This result is treated as
a relevant warning. A global positive association can be produced by spatial
proximity or degree structure, and therefore it cannot be used as the main
empirical claim of the manuscript.

\subsection{MICRONS stratified structure-function experiment}

The fifth experiment defines the primary endpoint as
\texttt{all\_pairs/readout\_location}. Synaptically connected directed pairs are
compared with non-connected pairs under random, distance-matched, and
degree-matched controls. The endpoint is accepted only if it remains positive
after matched controls, false discovery rate correction, and unit-cluster
bootstrap stability in the discovery cohort and in both hold-out cohorts.

Across the three cohorts, connected pairs show closer readout-location
similarity than matched non-connected pairs. The distance-matched delta is
0.0215 in the discovery cohort, 0.0210 in the first hold-out, and 0.0179 in the

respectively. Unit-cluster bootstrap intervals remain positive in every cohort
and for both distance- and degree-matched controls.

\subsection{Claim audit}

The publication freeze marks the replicated local MICRONS structure-function
claim as Q1-candidate evidence. At the same time, it blocks unsupported claims:
causal mechanism, complete mouse-brain digital twin, behavioural digital twin,
and Sensorium state-of-the-art predictor. Therefore, the final result is a
benchmark and claim-audit contribution, not a whole-brain simulation paper.
